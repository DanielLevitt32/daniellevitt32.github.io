The number of Missourians living in poverty decreased 2 percent to 908,394, according to estimated figures from the 2014 United States Census. St. Louis County saw the largest decrease, with 12,638 less people living below the poverty line taking its total to 93,882. However, St. Louis City saw 4,804 additional people living in poverty, taking its total to 88,571.

Other notable takeaways include:

  • 5 percent decrease in 0-17 year-olds living in poverty to 289,287
  • 48 percent of 0-17 year-olds in Shannon County are living in poverty
  • Missouri's median household income increased 3 percent to $48,288 in 2014, compared with $53,657 for the U.S. overall
  • Livingston County's median hosuehold income increase 23 percent to $47,937 from 2013 to 2014, the largest increase of any county
  • Mason County saw the largest decrease in median household income, decreasing 7 percent to $35,345
